Privacy Policy

Last updated: 3 July 2026

Tulip is built to be private by design. Your voice is transcribed entirely on your Mac — your audio and the text it becomes are never uploaded, stored on our servers, or seen by us. This policy explains the little that we do collect, and how to turn even that off.

The short version. Everything you dictate stays on your device. We can't read your transcriptions, and we never receive your audio. The only data that ever leaves your Mac is optional, anonymous usage counts you can switch off — plus your email & payment when you buy a license, handled by our payments provider.

1. Who we are

Tulip is a native macOS voice-dictation app made by Magically AI Inc. (“Tulip,” “we,” “us”), distributed at tulip.so. For any privacy question, contact info@magicallyhq.com.

2. Your dictation never leaves your device

Tulip records audio only while you hold or toggle the dictation key. That audio is transcribed on-device using speech-recognition models that run locally on your Mac (Apple’s on-device frameworks and bundled CoreML models). Optional smart formatting also runs on-device — either Apple Intelligence or Tulip’s built-in rule-based formatter. As a result:

  • Your audio is never transmitted to us or any third party, and is discarded from memory after transcription — we don’t save recordings.
  • Your transcribed text is never sent to us. It goes only where you put it: pasted at your cursor, and into your local dictation history on your Mac.
  • Your dictation history and custom dictionary are stored locally on your device, under your user account. We have no copy.

3. Anonymous usage analytics (optional, opt-out)

To understand how Tulip is used and where to improve it, the app can send anonymous, aggregate usage events. This is on by default but easy to turn off — during onboarding, or anytime in Settings → General → “Help improve Tulip.” When it’s off, the app makes no analytics network calls at all.

These events are processed on our behalf by PostHog (United States). They are tied only to a random, per-install identifier that is not linked to your name, email, or license.

What we collect

CategoryExamples
Usage countsApp launches, number of dictations, cancellations, onboarding completion
PerformanceLength of audio dictated (in seconds), transcription latency, word count of the result
ConfigurationWhich language and formatting style is selected, whether formatting is on, which engine ran
EnvironmentApp version, macOS version, Mac model, processor architecture, and coarse country (derived from IP, which is then discarded)

What we never collect

  • ✕ The content of your dictations — no transcripts, ever.
  • ✕ Your audio or recordings.
  • ✕ Your clipboard, keystrokes, screen contents, file names, or the apps you dictate into.
  • ✕ Any personal identity — no name, email, account, or precise location tied to usage.

Website analytics

Separately from the app, this website uses Cloudflare Web Analytics to count page views. It is privacy-first by design: it sets no cookies, uses no fingerprinting, and does not track you across sites. It records only aggregate page-visit data (such as page URL, referrer, and coarse country). This applies to the website only — it has nothing to do with the app or your dictation.

4. Purchases and licensing

When you buy Tulip, the checkout and payment are handled by our merchant of record, Lemon Squeezy. They collect and process your email address and payment details to complete the sale and issue your license — Tulip never sees your full payment information. Your license key is stored locally on your device and validated as needed. Please see Lemon Squeezy’s privacy policy for how they handle payment data.

5. Software updates

Tulip checks for updates using Sparkle, which requests our update feed at tulip.so/appcast.xml and downloads releases from our public distribution host. Like any web request, these include your IP address and app/OS version in transit; we don’t use them to build a profile of you. You can disable automatic update checks in the app.

6. Diagnostic logs

Tulip can keep a local diagnostic log to help troubleshoot problems. It is off by default, stored only on your Mac, and never contains the text of your dictations. Nothing is sent to us unless you deliberately enable logging, reproduce an issue, and choose to send us the file for support.

7. Data retention

Because on-device data (transcripts, history, dictionary, logs) lives only on your Mac, you control it — delete it anytime from within the app or by removing the app’s data. Anonymous analytics events are retained by PostHog under our account for as long as they’re useful for product analysis; they contain no information that identifies you.

8. Your choices & rights

  • Turn off analytics at any time in Settings — no data is sent thereafter.
  • Delete your local data (history, dictionary, logs) from within the app.
  • Depending on where you live (e.g. the EEA/UK under GDPR, or California under the CCPA/CPRA), you may have rights to access, correct, or delete personal data we hold. Because our analytics are anonymous and we don’t hold accounts, we usually have no personal data tied to you; for purchase data, contact Lemon Squeezy or us. Email info@magicallyhq.com and we’ll help.

9. Children

Tulip is not directed to children under 13 (or the equivalent minimum age in your country), and we do not knowingly collect personal data from them.

10. International users

Our analytics processor operates in the United States, so anonymous usage events may be processed there. By using Tulip you understand that this limited, anonymous data may be handled in the U.S.

11. Changes to this policy

We may update this policy as Tulip evolves. We’ll revise the “Last updated” date above, and material changes will be reflected here at tulip.so/privacy.html.

12. Contact

Questions about privacy? Email info@magicallyhq.com.
